## How to Use

### Step 1: Account Setup & Credit Management
1. Visit [klingai.com](https://klingai.com) and click "Sign Up" (supports email, Google, or Apple ID)
2. Verify your email to activate the free tier (100 credits/month)
3. **Critical Tip**: Each 5-second video consumes 25 credits. A 10-second video = 50 credits. Track usage via the "Credits" tab in your dashboard
4. Upgrade to Pro ($19.99/month) for 800 credits (allowing 32 x 10-second videos) or Enterprise for custom quotas

### Step 2: Creating Your First Video (Text-to-Video)
1. Click "Create" > "Text-to-Video"
2. **Craft effective prompts** using this formula:  
   `[Subject] + [Action] + [Environment] + [Physics detail] + [Style]`  
   *Example: "A red apple falling through glass, slow motion, with realistic shattering physics, cinematic lighting, Anime style"*
3. Adjust settings:
   - *Duration*: 5s (free) or 10/30s (Pro)
   - *Aspect Ratio*: 9:16 (TikTok/Reels), 16:9 (YouTube)
   - *Style*: Select from presets (avoid "Realistic" for physics-heavy scenes as it increases processing time)
4. Click "Generate" – processing takes 2-8 minutes depending on complexity

### Step 3: Image-to-Video Workflow
1. Click "Create" > "Image-to-Video"
2. Upload a high-resolution image (min 1024x1024px)
3. **Add motion direction** in the prompt:  
   *"Make the river flow left, add gentle wind movement to trees, cinematic"*
4. Use the "Motion Intensity" slider (0-100%) to control animation strength – set to 60-80% for natural physics
5. Generate and review the animated result

### Step 4: Editing & Optimization
1. **Trim** unnecessary footage using the timeline slider (Pro feature)
2. **Adjust speed** for dramatic slow-mo (e.g., 0.5x for physics-heavy moments like explosions)
3. **Apply style presets** consistently across multiple videos for brand alignment
4. **Export** as MP4 (1080p) – note: free tier adds a small Kling watermark

### Pro Tips for Physics Simulation
- **For fluid motion**: Use phrases like "slow motion," "fluid dynamics," or "viscous flow" in prompts
- **For collisions**: Specify "realistic shattering," "bouncing physics," or "deformation on impact"
- **Avoid overcomplication**: Physics simulations fail with >3 interacting elements (e.g., "a ball hitting a glass breaking into water" often errors)
- **Leverage negative prompts**: Add "no distortion, no unnatural movement" to improve stability
