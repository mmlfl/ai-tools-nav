## Overview

The AI search landscape has evolved dramatically, blending traditional search engines with generative AI, coding assistants, and multimodal capabilities. Two platforms that represent very different philosophies are **Tiangong AI** (天工 AI) and **You.com**. Tiangong AI, developed by Chinese tech company Kunlun Tech, is an all-in-one AI creation and search platform that tightly integrates web search, writing, coding, and creative tools into a single free service. In contrast, You.com is a privacy-first AI search engine built in the United States, giving users granular control over AI models and the ability to search across web pages, images, videos, and code simultaneously.

While both tools fall under the “search” category, their target audiences, feature sets, and pricing models are worlds apart. Tiangong AI is deeply embedded in the Chinese-language ecosystem, offering a comprehensive, no-cost solution for everyday tasks. You.com, on the other hand, appeals to a global audience that values data privacy, model transparency, and multimodal search. This comparison will break down the key differences in features, pricing, and ideal use cases to help you decide which tool fits your needs in 2026.

## Feature Comparison

The table below highlights the core differences in functionality between Tiangong AI and You.com. Because Tiangong AI is designed as a unified platform and You.com as a modular, privacy-centric search engine, their strengths diverge significantly.

| Feature | Tiangong AI | You.com |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Core Search Scope** | AI‑enhanced web search with a focus on Chinese‑language results. Integrates Kunlun’s own search index and third‑party sources. | Privacy‑first search across the open web, news, images, videos, and code repositories. Uses a combination of proprietary and external indexes. |
| **AI Model Flexibility** | Uses a proprietary set of models (likely Kunlun’s own LLMs) for search, writing, and coding. No user choice of model. | Multi‑model switching: users can select from GPT‑4o, Claude 3, Gemini, Llama, and others for chat and search responses. |
| **Multimodal Search** | Primarily text‑based search; image and video search are not prominently featured. | Simultaneous search across web, images, videos, news, and code. Results are unified in one interface. |
| **Privacy & Data Handling** | No explicit privacy‑first claim; subject to Chinese data regulations. User data handling is not transparently detailed. | Strong privacy focus: no tracking, no personal data collection, anonymous search, encrypted queries. Does not build user profiles. |
| **Content Creation Tools** | Built‑in AI writing assistant, code generation, and creative tools (e.g., article outlines, social media copy). Acts as an all‑in‑one workspace. | Writing modes (blog, email, essay) and a code‑specific assistant. However, it is primarily a search engine, not a full‑fledged content creation suite. |
| **Language Support** | Optimized for Chinese (Simplified). English and other languages are supported but secondary. | Primarily English, with multi‑language support for search queries and AI responses. |
| **Platform Accessibility** | Web‑based platform; mobile app availability is uncertain. | Web, iOS, Android apps, and a browser extension for quick access. |
| **Integration & Ecosystem** | Tightly integrated with Kunlun’s ecosystem (e.g., gaming, content). No third‑party API or plugin marketplace. | Offers an API for developers and integrates with popular tools via a growing plugin system. |

## Pricing Comparison

Pricing is one of the most striking differences between the two. Tiangong AI is entirely free, while You.com follows a freemium model with paid tiers that unlock advanced capabilities and higher usage limits.

| Plan | Tiangong AI | You.com |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Free Tier** | Full access to all features – search, writing, coding – with no usage caps. | Limited searches per day, basic AI model access, no file uploads. Ad‑supported. |
| **Entry‑Level Paid** | None | **Pro** – $20/month (or $15/month billed annually). Unlimited searches, priority access to advanced models (GPT‑4o, Claude 3), file upload support, and no ads. |
| **Mid‑Tier / Team** | None | **Team** – $30/user/month. All Pro features plus shared workspaces, admin controls, and collaborative search. |
| **Enterprise** | None | Custom pricing. Dedicated support, SSO, advanced security, and usage‑based agreements. |

Tiangong AI’s completely free model is made possible by Kunlun Tech’s broader business strategy, whereas You.com monetizes through subscriptions to maintain its privacy‑first, ad‑free experience at the paid tiers.

## Use Cases

### When to Choose Tiangong AI

- **Chinese‑Speaking Users Who Need an All‑in‑One Tool**  
  Tiangong AI is purpose‑built for Chinese users. If your primary language is Chinese and you want a single platform to search the web, draft documents, write code, and generate creative content, Tiangong AI’s unified experience is hard to beat.

- **Cost‑Sensitive Individuals and Students**  
  Because every feature is free, Tiangong AI is an excellent choice for students, freelancers, or anyone who wants a capable AI assistant without a subscription fee.

- **Users Inside China’s Internet Ecosystem**  
  The search results are tailored to the Chinese web, making it more relevant for local news, services, and content. If you operate primarily within China’s digital landscape, Tiangong AI will feel more native.

- **Those Who Prefer a Simplified, No‑Choice AI Experience**  
  Tiangong AI automatically selects the AI model for each task, removing the need to compare or switch models. This can be a pro for users who want a straightforward, no‑fuss assistant.

### When to Choose You.com

- **Privacy‑Conscious Users**  
  You.com’s entire architecture is built around user privacy. If you refuse to be tracked, profiled, or served targeted ads, You.com’s free and paid tiers offer a level of anonymity that Tiangong AI does not guarantee.

- **Global Researchers and Multilingual Professionals**  
  With support for multiple languages and the ability to search across different modalities (code, images, videos), You.com is better suited for international research, academic work, and technical documentation searches.

- **Developers and Power Users Who Want Model Flexibility**  
  The ability to switch between GPT‑4o, Claude 3, Gemini, and open‑source models gives you control over the style and accuracy of AI responses. This is critical when you need to compare outputs or use a specific model for a task.

- **Teams That Require Collaboration**  
  You.com’s Team plan adds shared workspaces and admin tools, making it a viable option for small businesses or research groups that want a privacy‑safe collaborative search environment.

## Verdict & Recommendation

**Tiangong AI** is a powerful, free, all-in-one platform that excels in the Chinese-language market. It combines search, writing, and coding into a seamless experience, making it an excellent daily driver for Chinese speakers who want a no-cost AI assistant. However, its lack of transparency around privacy, limited multimodal search, and heavy localization mean it is not the best fit for privacy‑sensitive or global users.

**You.com** sets the standard for privacy‑first AI search. Its multi‑model flexibility, multimodal search capabilities, and team features make it the superior choice for privacy‑conscious individuals, developers, and international professionals. The freemium model does introduce a paywall for heavy usage, but the free tier remains useful for casual searching.

**Final Recommendation:**  
- If you are a Chinese speaker looking for a free, integrated AI search and creation tool, **Tiangong AI** is an outstanding option.  
- If privacy, model choice, and multimodal search are your priorities, and you are comfortable with a subscription for advanced features, **You.com** is the clear winner.

For users who fall outside these clear‑cut profiles, consider your language needs, privacy tolerance, and willingness to pay. As the AI search space continues to mature, both platforms will undoubtedly evolve, but for now, the choice comes down to ecosystem and values.
