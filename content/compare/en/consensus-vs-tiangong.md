## Overview

In a digital landscape flooded with information, finding reliable answers quickly is more critical than ever. Two AI-powered search tools have emerged to tackle this challenge from very different angles. **Consensus** is a specialized academic search engine that mines over 200 million peer‑reviewed research papers to extract and synthesize scientific findings. It is built for students, researchers, and professionals who need evidence‑based answers, not just web links. On the other side, **Tiangong AI** (天工 AI), developed by Kunlun Tech, is a multi‑functional large‑model assistant that combines AI search with writing, coding, image generation, and even music creation—all in a single, free platform. While both tools fall under the “search” category, their philosophies, target audiences, and capabilities couldn’t be more distinct.

Consensus acts like a research librarian that reads papers for you, delivering concise summaries, citation‑backed claims, and a unique “Consensus Meter” that visualizes the degree of scientific agreement on a topic. Tiangong AI, by contrast, behaves like a Swiss Army knife: you can ask it a factual question, request a poem, debug code, generate an image, or analyze a PDF—all within the same chat interface. This comparison will dissect their features, pricing, and ideal use cases to help you decide which tool deserves a spot in your workflow.

## Feature Comparison

The table below highlights the core differences between Consensus and Tiangong AI across several key dimensions.

| Feature | Consensus | Tiangong AI |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **Primary Focus** | Academic research and evidence synthesis | General AI search and multi‑functional creation |
| **Data Sources** | 200M+ peer‑reviewed papers, scientific journals | Web search, proprietary large language model, multimodal databases |
| **Search Summarization** | Extracts key findings directly from papers; provides in‑line citations | AI‑generated answers with clickable source links; can read and summarize linked pages |
| **Consensus Meter** | Yes – shows how many papers support, refute, or are neutral on a query | No |
| **Multilingual Support** | Primarily English; limited support for other languages | Native Chinese, strong English, and several other languages |
| **Additional Tools** | Study snapshots, citation generator, ChatGPT plugin, bookmarks | AI writing, coding assistant, image generation, music composition, document analysis, voice input |
| **Accuracy & Bias** | High for scientific claims, but limited by the scope and quality of indexed papers | General web accuracy; may surface unverified sources or hallucinate on niche topics |
| **User Interface** | Clean, research‑oriented dashboard with filters for study type, journal, etc. | Chat‑style interface with a multi‑tool dashboard; supports long‑context conversations |

Both tools leverage large language models to interpret queries, but the underlying philosophy is fundamentally different. Consensus is laser‑focused on verifiability: every claim is tied to a specific academic paper, and the “Consensus Meter” gives users a quick sense of the scientific landscape. Tiangong AI, meanwhile, prioritizes breadth and convenience, offering a seamless blend of search and creation that feels like a personal AI assistant. It can generate a Python script, then immediately search the web for debugging tips, then compose a cover letter—all in one session.

A notable strength of Consensus is its ability to handle nuanced research questions such as “Does creatine improve cognitive function in sleep‑deprived adults?” and return a curated list of relevant studies with key takeaways. Tiangong AI can answer similar questions by synthesizing web content, but it may mix academic sources with blog posts, news articles, and forum discussions, making it harder to gauge reliability without manual verification. On the flip side, Tiangong AI’s multimodal capabilities (image, music, code) make it far more versatile for creative and technical tasks that Consensus simply doesn’t address.

## Pricing Comparison

One of the starkest contrasts between the two tools is pricing. Consensus operates on a freemium model, while Tiangong AI is currently entirely free.

| Plan | Consensus | Tiangong AI |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Free Tier** | 20 searches per month; basic features (study snapshots, limited filters) | Unlimited access to all features: search, writing, coding, image/music generation, document analysis |
| **Premium (Individual)** | $11.99/month or $9.99/month billed annually; unlimited searches, advanced filters, Consensus Meter, bookmarks, export options | N/A – entirely free |
| **Teams / Enterprise** | Custom pricing for shared workspaces, admin controls, and API access | N/A |
| **Advertisements** | Ad‑free on all plans | Ad‑free |

For academic power users, Consensus’s Premium plan is a worthwhile investment—unlimited searches and the full Consensus Meter can dramatically speed up literature reviews. However, the free tier’s 20‑search cap is restrictive and pushes serious users toward a paid subscription. Tiangong AI’s completely free model is exceptionally generous, especially considering the breadth of its creative and analytical tools. It remains to be seen whether Kunlun Tech will introduce a paid tier in the future, but for now, users can explore the entire platform without spending a dime.

## Use Cases

### When to Choose Consensus

- **Academic Research & Literature Reviews:** Students, PhD candidates, and professors can quickly locate relevant papers, extract findings, and gauge scientific consensus without manually sifting through databases like PubMed or Google Scholar.
- **Evidence‑Based Decision Making:** Healthcare professionals, policy analysts, and journalists can verify health claims, social science hypotheses, or environmental statements with direct citations to peer‑reviewed studies.
- **Fact‑Checking & Debunking:** The Consensus Meter instantly reveals whether a claim (e.g., “Vitamin D prevents colds”) enjoys broad scientific support or remains controversial.
- **Citation Generation:** Built‑in tools for generating references in multiple formats save time when compiling bibliographies.
- **ChatGPT Integration:** The official Consensus plugin for ChatGPT allows users to inject academic evidence directly into their conversational workflows.

Consensus is not ideal for general knowledge queries, breaking news, or creative tasks. Its database, while vast, is limited to scholarly publications, so questions about pop culture, current events, or practical how‑to guides will yield poor results.

### When to Choose Tiangong AI

- **Everyday AI Search & Assistance:** Users who want a single tool to answer questions, summarize web pages, and provide up‑to‑date information will find Tiangong AI’s search‑and‑chat interface intuitive and fast.
- **Content Creation & Brainstorming:** The integrated AI writing assistant can draft articles, social media posts, marketing copy, and even poetry. Combined with image and music generation, it’s a one‑stop creative suite.
- **Coding & Technical Help:** Developers can ask for code snippets, debugging advice, or explanations of complex algorithms, all while using the search function to pull in documentation or Stack Overflow threads.
- **Multilingual Users:** Native Chinese speakers, or anyone working across languages, benefit from Tiangong AI’s strong bilingual support and culturally aware responses.
- **Students & Lifelong Learners (General):** While not a replacement for deep academic research, Tiangong AI is excellent for quick explanations, homework help, and learning new topics through interactive dialogue.

Tiangong AI’s main limitation is the reliability of its sources. When answering scientific or medical questions, it may draw from less authoritative websites, and it lacks a built‑in mechanism to show the weight of evidence. For tasks that demand rigorous, citation‑backed accuracy, Consensus remains the superior choice.

## Verdict & Recommendation

Choosing between Consensus and Tiangong AI comes down to a simple question: **Do you need academic rigor or creative versatility?**

If your work or study revolves around scientific evidence, academic writing, or fact‑based decision making, Consensus is the clear winner. Its curated database of peer‑reviewed papers, the innovative Consensus Meter, and seamless citation tools offer a level of trust and efficiency that general‑purpose AI search engines cannot match. The free tier is enough to test the waters, but anyone serious about research will quickly see the value in the Premium plan.

On the other hand, if you want a free, all‑in‑one AI companion that can search the web, write an essay, debug code, generate an image, and even compose a melody, Tiangong AI is a remarkable offering. Its zero‑cost access and broad feature set make it an attractive option for casual users, content creators, and developers who value versatility over academic depth. The platform’s Chinese roots also give it an edge in multilingual scenarios, though English support is robust.

In an ideal workflow, these tools complement each other perfectly. Use Consensus when you need to anchor an argument in scientific literature; turn to Tiangong AI for brainstorming, drafting, and exploring creative possibilities. Together, they cover a wide spectrum of modern AI‑powered search and creation needs.
